AIM AND OBJECTIVE

At S.Vatsalya Academy, our mission is to create a nurturing and inclusive learning environment where every student can excel academically, socially, and emotionally. We aim to achieve this through a commitment to academic excellence, providing rigorous educational programs that challenge and inspire our students. Our holistic approach to education emphasizes the development of character, creativity, and critical thinking skills, ensuring that students are well-prepared for the challenges of the future. We celebrate diversity and promote inclusivity, fostering a supportive community where all students feel valued and empowered. By actively engaging with our local community and encouraging students to become responsible global citizens, we instill a sense of social responsibility and leadership. We equip our students with the 21st-century skills necessary for success in an ever-changing world, while also fostering strong partnerships with parents and guardians to support student learning. Committed to continuous improvement, we regularly assess our practices and programs to ensure that we are meeting the evolving needs of our students and community, ultimately striving to provide an exceptional educational experience that prepares students for success in school, careers, and life.

PARENT'S AND TEACHERS MEETING

A Parents/Teacher meeting is held on a regular basis to provided platform for parents to have an open discussion with the concerned teacher, regarding the progress and performance of their child. Parents/ Teacher Meetings are usually held after an examination to facilitate viewing/signing of the report cards by the parent. Hence, it is necessary for parents to attend every parents/ Teacher Meeting. Parents Teacher Meeting (Other than scheduled dates) is subject to prior permission of principal. Parents/ guardians are strictly forbidden to enter the classes and interview the teachers on any other ordinary school day. In case, there is need for dealing with a problem on an ordinary school day, this must be done through the principal only.
